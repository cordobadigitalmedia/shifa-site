aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Living a Balanced Life</strong></p><p>We are continuously struggling and often failing in meeting life’s changing demands with respect to ourselves, our families, our jobs, our friends, our neighbors, our community and our faith. We often become overwhelmed with these changing needs and demands that stress us out and at times leave us depressed, confused and helpless. Many becomes overly focused on one aspect of life thereby ignoring the other more important aspects. For example, becoming solely preoccupied with our work, our career golas, having fun, etc., and as a result ignoring the needs of our soul, our spouses and children and the obligations to Allah.</p><p>This workshop is designed to:<br> 1. To identify some of the Life Pressures and Demands in our Life.<br> 2. To understand how people are handling them generally now, and<br> 3. And to lean how to best handle all these pressures through Living a Balanced Life.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458768103-2-4" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-2" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Effective Communication</strong></p><p>There are literally thousands of books, papers and articles written on the importance of effective communication in our relationships. How often people complain about having communication problems. There would hardly be a person who would deny the need for good communication, but sadly most people are lacking the skills required for effective communication.</p><p>This workshop is designed with the following objectives:<br> 1. Understanding the process of communication.<br> 2. What are the factors that make it effective or ineffective.<br> 3. What skills are necessary for effective communication.<br> 4. Creating a family environment that promotes open and honest communication, and<br> 5. How we can promote mutual trust necessary for effective communication.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458805047-2-10" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-3" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="false" style="display: table-cell;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Conflict Resolution</strong></p><p>This workshop is second in the series on Effective Communication. Effective Communication must be learned first before being able to resolve conflicts effectively.</p><p>This workshop is designed to assist the participants:<br> 1. In learning to identify the nature of conflicts;<br> 2. In sharing their present conflicts and consequences thereof; and<br> 3. To provides an opportunity to learn effective conflict resolution skills based on self-awareness, communication skills, and Islamic principles.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458836774-3-10" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-4" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Anger Management&nbsp;</strong></p><p>Allah (s.w.t.) has created ‘anger’ so that it may serve as a weapon to defend you from harmful things; and He created desire to help you move towards what is<br> beneficial for you. But, when these two go to an extreme, they become harmful and like a fire in your heart, which fills your brain with smoke and darkens the sources of intelligence and wisdom rendering you unable to think of and understand long range consequences of your actions. This is why our elders<br> have called them ‘Wisdom Robbers’.</p><p>This workshop is designed to assist participants<br> 1. Understand the nature of anger – its purpose, causes,expressions and effects.<br> 2. To learn what make them angry and why; and<br> 3. To manage and express it without any damage to self and others.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458861990-4-8" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-5" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Conflicts and Struggles of Muslim Women (for sisters)&nbsp;</strong></p><p>This workshop is designed for the Muslim sisters with the following goals.<br> 1. To provide Muslim sisters an opportunity to voice and share their conrflicts and struggles.<br> 2. To understand some of the current and historical issues leading to these conflicts and struggles.<br> 3. To learn about the nature of men and women from an Islamic perspective.<br> 4. To discuss the roles, rights and responsibilities of men and women in Islam, and<br> 5. To learn how and when to struggle and for what, and get rid of the conflicts.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458885224-5-10" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-6" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Choice – Do we have any?</strong></p><p>How many times do we hear people complain, “I have no choice”, or “I had no choice”. We often make ourselves believe that we must keep suffering, we must keep trying more of the same that we already know does not work, because we see no other choice. Do we really not have any choice in anything?</p><p>The objectives of this workshop are:<br> 1. To understand the concept of Choices.<br> 2. To understand the importance of making choices and their implications and consequences in this life and the life hereafter.<br> 3. To understand our freedoms and limitations with respect to making choices.<br> 4. To learn the proper process of making choices, and<br> 5. To know how there is always a choice.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1428445330683-12-0" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-7" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>We are always Changing, but are we growing?</strong></p><p>A man claims to be short tempered. He says this runs in his genes. His father, uncle, etc., were all short tempered. He gets violent with his wife and children, but claims that he loves them, that he cannot change how he is. Interestingly, the same man, when at work, does not lose his temper, does not get verbally or physically abusive, and is often known as a nice man. So, what changes him to be a genetically angry and short tempered man at home?</p><p>The fact is that we are continuously changing, but often unconsciously until somethings happens to make us notice what we have become. This workshop is designed to:<br> 1. Help the participant realize that they are all changing all the time.<br> 2. To realize that the on-going unconscious and uncontrolled change process almost always results in dehumanizing us, and<br> 3. To learn to take charge of the change process actively so that positive changes may occur in ourselves ensuring continuous growth.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458910079-6-2" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-8" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Dealing with Depression and Anxiety</strong></p><p>Depression and anxiety are very common emotional problems. Literally, millions of men and women suffer from these around the world. These are often treated with psychotropic medications. Unless the root causes of these problems are understood and appropriately dealt with, many people will become totally dependent upon the medications for the relief of the symptoms.</p><p>This workshop is designed to assist those who are suffering from these problems.</p><p>The objectives of this workshop are:</p><p>1. To understand the nature, symptoms, and causes of depression<br> 2. To provide an opportunity for the participants to share their personal experiences with these issues.<br> 3. To learn the difference between Depression and Anxiety as a mental disorders, and normal sadness and worries which are also often labeled as Depression and Anxiety.<br> 4. To learn what makes one vulnerable to these mental/emotional problems.and<br> 5. To learn how to effectively prevent and deal with these issues.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458939646-7-3" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-9" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Stress Management</strong></p><p>Stress takes it toll on our physical, mental, emotional and spiritual life. If stress is not properly dealt with, it may lead to more severe medical and emotional problems. This workshop deals with:</p><p>1. Understanding the nature of stress – what it is all about, and what causes it.<br> 2. It’s effect on our body, mind and soul,<br> 3. What are some of the most effective ways of dealing with it and avoiding it?</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424458980431-8-7" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-10" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Mental Health and Mental Illness</strong></p><p>There is a lot of misunderstanding and misconception about mental health and mental illness amongst the Muslim communities. Often psychological and emotional problems are seen as a sign of weakness and weak Iman – something to hide and shameful. Some chalk these issues out to magic (sahar) and the influcence of Jinns. Consequently, they don’t seek appropriate help. Either they silently keep suffering, attribute it to their fate, or they seek help from the wrong sources for the wrong reasons.</p><p>The objective of this workshops are:</p><p>1. To explain the concept of Mental Health and Mental Illness.<br> 2. To remove the prevailing misconceptions – Good Muslims won’t suffer from Mental Illnesses.<br> 3. To Understand the difference between normal life issues we all suffer from within ourselves and in our relationships and true mental, psychological, behavioral and emotional problems.<br> 4. To encourage the participants to share their experiences with these issues, and<br> 5. To learn what is counseling and psychotherapy all about, and how does it help relieve these issues or manage them appropriately, and</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424459019823-9-7" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-11" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Time Management</strong></p><p>Why do so many people complain about not having enough time and not being able to manage time properly? Interestingly, many of them are able to find time for extracurricular activities, but not for some important things in life, such as getting the work done in a timely fashion, spending some quality time the family, and fulfill the requirements of their faith.</p><p>This workshop is designed with the goals of:</p><p>1. Helping the audience reflect upon and understand what factors play a role in making it difficult to manage time properly.<br> 2. How to remove the barriers in effectively managing their time, and<br> 3. Giving them some strategies to manage time properly, get done what needs to be done in a timely fashion, and reduce the stress of always rushing to do everything last minute.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424459054550-10-9" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-12" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Unique Struggles of the New-Comers (Reverts) to Islam</strong></p><p>This workshop is designed especially for the Reverts to Islam. It addresses the issues and struggles that are unique to them and are often get ignored by the larger Muslim community.</p><p>The objectives of the workshop are:</p><p>1. To have the participants share their on-going struggles, and<br> 2. T provide guidance in how to effectively deal with some of the following oft mentioned issues.<br> – Learning and properly understanding the new faith.<br> – Differentiating between Culture and Religion.<br> – Effectively dealing with the variations in the interpretation of beliefs and practice.<br> – Coming to terms with the hypocrisy in practice.<br> – Re-establishing a good support system.<br> – Selecting a spouse – Warnings and Cautions, and<br> – Marriage and the rights of the husband and wife.</p><p>It is a full day workshop, or it may be broken down in to two half day workshops.</p></div></div></div><div id="tab-1424459078537-11-3" class="wpb_tab ui-tabs-panel wpb_ui-tabs-hide vc_clearfix ui-widget-content ui-corner-bottom" aria-labelledby="ui-id-13" role="tabpanel" aria-hidden="true" style="display: none;"><div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element  "><div class="wpb_wrapper"><p><strong>Learning to Move on – Dealing with Trauma&nbsp;</strong></p><p>This workshop is designed to assist the Muslim families and communities in effectively dealing with traumatic events in a family or in a community. Every now and then some scary and threatening events happen in a community which affects the entire community. Our Muslim communities generally do not have any support services readily available to console and effectively guide the family/community members through it and to help them move on.</p><p>The objectives of this workshop are:</p><p>1. To provide an opportunity to the participants to freely express their perceptions and emotions of the recent traumatic experience.<br> 2. To help them understand the nature of Trauma and its effects and possible outcomes on a person’s life, and<br> 3. To arrange for individual assistance for the more severely affected persons, and<br> 4. To guide through the healing and moving on process.</p></div></div></div><div class="wpb_tour_next_prev_nav clearfix"> <span class="wpb_prev_slide"><a href="#prev" title="Previous slide">Previous slide</a></span> <span class="wpb_next_slide"><a href="#next" title="Next slide">Next slide</a></span></div></div>
